Blade Runner 2049 Review

Blade Runner 2049 (2017)

Rotton Tomatoes: 89%

My rating: 4/5

Sypnosis

Set in 2049, thirty years after the events of the first film (Blade Runner) a young LAPD officer K, a new breed replicant "blade runner", unearths a long buried secret: a replicant gave birth to a real human. This leads K on a mission to find who the child might be, even questioning if he could be the child himself, and Richard Deckard, a former blade runner who has disappeared since the events of the first film.


As a sci-fi film, this was one of the films that impacted me the most. It became a very memorable experience. To this day, the quote that I still remember is: "Dying for the right cause is the most human thing we can do." It's a powerful message that resonates with the themes of identity and humanity in the film.

Visually, the film is stunning. Every scene is a unique blend of distopian and futuristic aesthetics. Roger Deakins' cinematography is breathtaking, creating a world that is both beautiful and haunting. The use of lighting and color adds depth to the story, making it an immersive experience.

Performances from Ryan Gosling, Ana de Armas and Harrison Ford feel very natural and authenic. Gosling delivers a compelling performance as K, capturing the character's internal struggle and emotional depth. Ana de Armas brings warmth and vulnerability to her role as Joi, while Harrison Ford reprises his role as Deckard with a sense of gravitas and nostalgia.

The soundtrack by the legendary composer Hans Zimmer is also worth mentioning. The music perfeclty complements the mood and atmosphere of the film. The use of electronic elements in the score really sets the mood of a scene. The most notable track is "Tears in the rain" where K realizes his fate as Deckard leaves him. The music adds an emotional weight to the scene, making it even more impactful.
